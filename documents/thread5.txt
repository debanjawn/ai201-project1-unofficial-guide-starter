COMMENT by thisfilmkid:
Yes, this threatens your financial aid. Best course of action: speak to financial aid about the Waiver of Good Academic Standing. Or, an appeal.

Then again, too many questions to give an answer directly. If you're a freshman, financial aid can waive your first semester and allow you to retake the failed classes, assuming you'll pass them, without financial penalties.

Worst case scenario: take a loan for next semester. Get your grades up. Pass. And you should be eligible for financial aid again.

Review the policy: https://www.cuny.edu/wp-content/uploads/sites/4/page-assets/financial-aid/information-resources/staff-resources/sam-manuals/SAM05-2016-1.pdf

COMMENT by ScallionWall:
Financial aid, among its many rules, is dependent on you making satisfactory academic progress. They won't pay if you're not on pace for a degree.
You likely won't receive a specific answer here as there's a lot of rules and everyone's situation is unique.

My advice: Speak to a financial aid counselor.
Then consider CUNY'S F grade policy, where you can retake a failed course at the same school. A grade of C or above will negate the F grade impact and really help your GPA recover.

COMMENT by ScallionWall:
The repeat policy does still apply. A new grade of C or above at the same school will negate the F grade impact (still on transcript). This works for up to 16 credits within CUNY.

Whether or not Grove/your major program will choose to count a failed attempt against you for their requirements is up to them. But your GPA would still recover from utilizing the F grade repeat policy.
